    CITATION

    Methodology

    The Ledger records what each local education agency reports to the California Department of Education, and publishes district figures only when they reproduce CDE's own published numbers exactly. It does not rank schools or characterize their choices.

    METHOD NOTES
    S-1The gate is exact. Every school district figure on this page is the Current Expense of Education (CDE's EDP 365: General Fund current operating expense — instruction, services, administration, plant — excluding food services, facilities construction, community-service and non-agency activities, and retiree-benefit objects, per CDE's formula). The pipeline recomputes it from the raw SACS general ledger and refuses to publish unless every district-year matches CDE's published figure to within five cents — in practice, to the cent. The function rows on each record sum exactly to that gated figure.
    S-2Comparison is districts only, per ADA. Average daily attendance is the standard education denominator, and the ADA here is the same figure CDE publishes beside its per-ADA statistic. County offices of education are records only, never compared — CDE itself excludes them from its per-ADA statistic, and their responsibilities (court and community schools, SELPAs, fiscal oversight, pass-through) are not a district's. Charter schools are records only as well. K-12 entities are never compared to or summed with cities, counties, special districts, or the state budget.
    S-3Three structural notes travel with district figures. Basic aid: districts whose local property tax exceeds their LCFF entitlement keep the excess, so per-ADA spending partly reflects tax-base geography rather than choices (the share of districts in this position is computed from CDE's LCFF data each run). Small districts: necessary-small-school funding floors make per-ADA figures structurally high below a few hundred ADA. Sponsored charters: a district whose separately-reporting charter ADA is large relative to its own is shown with that fact, because its record describes only the district-run schools.
    S-4Charters file three ways, and this page never double-counts them. Most charter spending (about 80% of charter ADA) is filed separately — those schools have their own records here, reconciled through CDE's rollups. Charters in an authorizer's Funds 09/62 sit outside the district's Current Expense figure and are shown as a separate line on the district record. Charters commingled in an authorizer's Fund 01 cannot be separated; the affected district records state the count and ADA on their face, and CDE's own published figure for those districts includes them.
    S-5This layer and the state budget see the same money. The overlap statement on this page is computed by the pipeline on every run from the ledger itself and the state layer's own data file. Named traps: the Education Protection Account is continuously appropriated outside the annual Budget Act but inside the state's General Fund display; STRS on-behalf contributions appear in both layers at different values; statewide LEA sums contain inter-LEA pass-throughs; and Proposition 98 is a K-14 guarantee that also counts property taxes — it is neither the state agency total nor total school funding. The two layers agree to roughly 1–3.5% and never to the dollar (enacted appropriations vs. year-end accrual actuals).
    S-6Scope and vintages. Fiscal years 2022-23 through 2024-25 (SACS archives exist back to 2003-04; the window extends by running the pipeline). Figures are unaudited actuals as filed. ROPs, SELPAs, and other education JPAs file to CDE and are inside the reconciliation rollups but are not shown as records in this version. Traditional-LEA filing completeness is 100% — every district and county office files, under AB 1200 oversight.
    SOURCES & RECORD
    Primary sources: CDE SACS unaudited actuals (www3.cde.ca.gov/fiscal-downloads, annual archives), CDE's published Current Expense of Education (the reconciliation target), and LCFF summary data (basic-aid flag, county-office ADA). The state budget page shows the K-12 agency's appropriations — the same money seen from the other direction, on a different accounting basis; see the overlap statement above.
    RECORD INTEGRITY
    Verify independently: download school-data.js and run python3 pipeline/verify_digest.py school-data.js — it recomputes the SHA-256 of the canonical JSON payload. Each district record also stores CDE's published figure beside the recomputed one, so the gate itself can be re-checked from the data file.
    Citations reproduce the exact view — entities, fiscal year, and unit. District dollar figures are exact as filed; per-ADA figures are rounded to the dollar.
