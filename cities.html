    Sources & method

    Figures are reported actual revenues and expenditures from the standardized annual financial reports every California city files with the State Controller's Office, published at bythenumbers.sco.ca.gov — retrospective figures, not budgets. Function figures cover governmental activities; ratepayer-funded enterprise operations (water, power, airports, harbors, hospitals, transit) are shown separately per city and excluded from comparisons, because cities differ in which they operate. Internal service funds and conduit financing are excluded from both blocks; every city-year total is reconciled against the Controller's published per-capita dataset before this page's data file is written. "Per resident" divides each figure by the population reported in the same filing. Police and fire service-provision notes derive from the State Controller's services checklist, whose most recent published vintage is FY 2015-16 — service arrangements may have changed since then. The additional flag on cities reporting under $5 per resident for a service is a heuristic backstop for the checklist's age, not a current-year survey of who provides the service. San Francisco is footnoted as the state's only consolidated city and county. Reports for a fiscal year are published the following year — run pipeline/fetch_city_data.py --write to refresh. The state budget view covers the enacted state budget from official data.
