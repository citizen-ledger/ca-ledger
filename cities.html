    Methodology

    The Ledger records what each city reports to the State Controller, mapped to the Controller's standardized functions. It does not rank cities or characterize their choices. Every figure comes from the official dataset described below and can be independently verified.

    METHOD NOTES
    M-1Scope is each city's governmental activities — the tax- and grant-funded functions in its annual report. This is broader than the general fund: it includes special-revenue, capital-project, and debt-service funds. Ratepayer-funded enterprise activities (water, power, airports, harbors, hospitals, transit) are recorded separately per city and excluded from comparisons, because cities differ in which they operate. Internal service funds and conduit financing are excluded from both blocks.
    M-2Functions follow the State Controller's standardized report categories; every source line maps to exactly one function (the crosswalk is in pipeline/fetch_city_data.py). Capital outlay and debt service are visible functions, not folded away — a single project or bond issue can move a small city's one-year total, and rows that swing more than ±40% year over year are footnoted.
    M-3Figures are reported actual revenues and expenditures as filed with the State Controller — retrospective figures, not budgets, and not audited by the Ledger. Fiscal years are labeled by ending year ("2023-24" ends June 30, 2024); reports for a fiscal year are published about a year later. Every city-year total is reconciled against the Controller's published per-capita dataset before this page's data file is written.
    M-4Per-resident figures divide by the population reported in the same State Controller filing — never a mixed vintage.
    M-5Service-structure notes (†) record where police or fire service is delivered or financed outside the city budget — for example, a city that contracts police to its county sheriff, or receives fire service from a special district. They derive from the State Controller's services checklist, whose most recent published vintage is FY 2015-16 — service arrangements may have changed since then. The supplementary flag on cities reporting under $5 per resident for a service is a heuristic backstop for the checklist's age, not a current-year survey of who provides the service.
    M-6San Francisco is California's only consolidated city and county; its report includes county functions (courts, jails, social services, elections) no other city provides, and it is footnoted wherever it appears. It files as a city and appears only in the Cities layer — never in the county figures, so it is counted exactly once.
    M-7The Counties layer covers the 57 counties that file with the State Controller, on the same basis and with the same reconciliation gate as cities: every county-year reproduces the Controller's published control total before publication. Counties provide municipal-type services chiefly to unincorporated areas, so each county carries a data-derived note showing the share of its residents who live outside any city, computed from the same filings' populations — per-resident differences partly reflect that responsibility share, not spending choices. Cities and counties are never compared to each other: a city and the county containing it spend on overlapping populations with different responsibilities, and the page's selection works within one layer at a time.
    SOURCES & RECORD
    Primary source: the State Controller's "By the Numbers" open-data portal (bythenumbers.sco.ca.gov) — City Expenditures (ju3w-4gxp), City Revenues (rrtv-rsj9), City Expenditures Per Capita (ykhf-vfsr, the reconciliation target), and the services checklist (tsz3-29gc). One new fiscal year per annual filing cycle. The state budget view shows enacted appropriations — a different accounting basis, stated on its own page.
