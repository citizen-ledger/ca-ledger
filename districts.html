Special districts

This page reports the measured condition of special district finance in California — the finding first, then a directory of every district on record, then each district's figures as filed. The order is deliberate: what can be verified leads, and what cannot be verified is labeled.

    Methodology

    This layer is deliberately not the evidentiary tier of the rest of the Ledger, and every surface of this page says so. The finding and the directory are verifiable; the dollar figures are as filed, and no design choice here is allowed to dress them up.

    METHOD NOTES
    D-1The tier, stated. Every state, city, and county figure on this site passes a hard reconciliation gate against an independently published control total before publication. No control-total dataset exists for special districts, so that gate is structurally impossible here — the one layer of the Ledger where this is true. District figures appear exactly as filed, with the caveat on the face of every record, every CSV, and every citation.
    D-2No per-resident figures, anywhere on this page. Special districts have no resident denominator — they serve connections, parcels, students, ratepayers, or service areas, and the filings carry no population field. Dividing district dollars by any population would fabricate a statistic, so the data file for this layer carries no population at all.
    D-3No comparison, no total. The two largest district types by count are legal forms (joint powers authorities, county service areas), not functions, so even within-type comparison would assert a likeness the data cannot support — none is offered. Dependent districts' finances also appear within the reporting of the city or county that governs them, so summing this layer would count the same dollars more than once — the Ledger publishes no total for it, and this layer is never compared to or summed with the state, city, or county layers.
    D-4Enterprise and governmental funds are kept apart, exactly as on the city and county pages: most as-filed district dollars sit in ratepayer-funded enterprise funds (water, sewer, hospitals, transit), which are not commensurable with tax-funded governmental activity.
    D-5Filing status is a fact, reported per district and per year. "Filed" means line items appear in the Controller's published datasets. "Filed late" and "Did not file" come from the Controller's published lists, which exist for fiscal years 2018-19 through 2023-24 — for 2016-17 and 2017-18 no list was published, and this page says "no list published" rather than guessing. The lists print district names cut at about 40 characters; they are matched to filers by normalized prefix and county, ambiguous rows are counted in the finding's method box and attached to nothing, and list entries with no line items in any year appear in the directory exactly as the Controller printed them.
    D-6The directory is the union of both source datasets. Some districts file revenue line items and no expenditures, or the reverse (measured in the finding's method box), so a district is "on record" if it appears in either. A few districts are spelled differently across filing years; spellings are merged when they are identical after punctuation normalization, in the same county, and never co-occur in a year — and the variants are kept on the record. Nothing stronger than that is merged.
    D-7No map. Special districts are not a Census geography, and no statewide boundary file exists from any state source. Rather than draw approximate territory, the layer ships without a map; each directory row links to the district's own page on the Controller's site instead.
